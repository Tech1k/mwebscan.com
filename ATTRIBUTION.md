## Requirement

Public products, hosted services, wallets, explorers, dashboards, APIs, research
tools, reports, or applications that use, display, redistribute, or derive results
from MWEBscan code, data, labels, scores, links, heuristics, methodology, or API
output must preserve reasonable attribution to the original project and author.

Acceptable attribution includes "Data from MWEBscan by Tech1k," "Powered by
MWEBscan," or similar wording, with a link to https://mwebscan.com or the upstream
repository.

Attribution must be reasonably visible to users where MWEBscan-derived results are
displayed (for example an About, Credits, Data Sources, API, Privacy Analysis, or
Methodology screen). It does not need to appear on a home screen or carry the
MWEBscan logo.

Attribution must not imply endorsement by MWEBscan or Tech1k unless written
permission is granted.

## API terms (short form)

For products consuming the public API, the simpler wording is:

> Attribution: Public products displaying MWEBscan data, links, scores, labels,
> heuristics, or API-derived results must include attribution such as "Data from
> MWEBscan by Tech1k" or "Powered by MWEBscan," with a link to mwebscan.com. Do not
> imply endorsement unless written permission is granted.

AGPL-3.0 Section 7(b) permits additional terms requiring preservation of reasonable
legal notices or author attributions. Per the FSF's guidance, this requirement is
intended to be modest and tied only to authorship and data-source credit; it is not
